About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington, and am fortunate to be advised advised by Zaid Harchaoui. Previously, I was an undergraduate and Master's student in the Department of Applied Mathemathics and Statistics at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Joshua T. Vogelstein and Carey Priebe.

I am broadly interested in machine learning and optimization. My current research focuses on distributionally robust optimization and applying graph neural networks to problems in neuroscience.
