Tutoring

I offer virtual tutoring for college mathematics courses, with specialties in linear algebra and statistics. Sessions are tailored to student needs, such as working through homework problems together or reviewing content. Sessions are recorded and sent to students to review on their own. Please contact me for more information!

Experience:

I received my Master's degree in applied mathematics from Johns Hopkins University (JHU) in 2019, and have since been a Ph.D. student in statistics at the University of Washington (UW). While at JHU, I was a tutor for student athletes and students from disadvantaged backgrounds as part of the Center for Student Success, in courses such as multivariate calculus, discrete mathematics, and Python programming. I have also been a teaching assistant for statistics and linear algebra courses at the Ph.D. level. Additionally, have taught my own course on mathematics preparation for engineering at JHU. Please see my teaching page for detailed experience.
